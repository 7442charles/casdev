The above format of the balance sheet has the following additional details:
i. Drawings – these are deducted from the capital amount since they reduce capital. They include cash or some other assets that the owner takes from the business for his own personal use.
ii. Net profit/ loss – this is the profit/ loss made within the period. It is extracted form the Trading, profit and loss statement.

2. THE TRADING PROFIT AND LOSS STATEMENT

This is a final financial statement that shows the gross and net profit/ loss that the business has made from its trading activities during a financial period. It is also referred to as an income statement/ statement of financial performance.

The determination of Gross profit/ loss and net profit/ loss follows the formulae below.
Gross profit/ loss = Net Sales - Cost of goods sold
Net sales = Total sales – Returns Inwards
Cost of goods sold = (Opening Stock + Purchases + Carriage Inwards – Returns Outwards – Closing Stock) Net profit = Gross profit + Other incomes – Expenses

The final formulae which is the basis of the trading profit and loss statement can therefore be stated as below:
Total Sales – Returns Inwards = Net Sales
Net sales – (Opening Stock + Purchases + carriage inwards – Returns Outwards – Closing Stock) = Gross Profit Gross profit + other incomes – Expenses = Net Profit

Note:
i. Returns inwards - these are goods that are returned to us by our customers due to various reasons. To arrive at the final/ Net sales figure, we deduct returns inwards from the total sales.
ii. Opening stock – this is the amount of stock that was available at the beginning of a financial period before any purchases were made.
iii. Purchases – these are goods bought for resale.
iv. Carriage inwards – this is the cost of bringing In purchases. Carriage inwards increases the cost of purchases.
v. Returns outwards – These are goods that we return to our suppliers and creditors. They reduce our purchases.
vi. Closing stock – this is the amount of stock that remains unsold at the end of a financial period
vii. Other incomes – trading refers to the buying and selling of goods to make profit. A business may however increase from its profits through other sources of incomes eg Rent income. These incomes are added to the gross profit before deducting expenses in order to realise the business’ net profit/ loss.
